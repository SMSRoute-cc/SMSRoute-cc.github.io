What Is the 10DLC Burden and Why Does It Matter?

US 10DLC (10-Digit Long Code) registration is a mandatory carrier framework for any business sending Application-to-Person (A2P) SMS over US local numbers. It requires:

  • Business identity verification (EIN, DUNS, or equivalent)
  • Campaign registration with The Campaign Registry (TCR)
  • Vetting fees per brand and per campaign (often $10–$50+ each)
  • Approval delays of 1–5 business days
  • Ongoing compliance with carrier rules and throughput limits

For developers, startups, privacy-conscious projects, or anyone sending internationally, this is a heavy gate. SMSRoute avoids it entirely by routing through international carriers that do not require 10DLC registration. Your messages reach US phones via non-US routes where the 10DLC framework does not apply.

How Does International Routing Work as a Compliant Alternative?

International routing means your SMS enters the US network from an overseas carrier partner. The US carriers treat it as international traffic, which is not subject to 10DLC registration. This is a standard, legal method used by many global providers. SMSRoute's adaptive multi-route delivery automatically selects the best international path for each message, with automatic failover if a route degrades. You get delivery to US numbers without ever touching the 10DLC system.

This approach is ideal for:

  • Developers who want to test or launch without compliance delays
  • Projects with low-to-moderate US volume (thousands to low millions per month)
  • Privacy-first applications where business registration is undesirable
  • International campaigns that also send to non-US recipients

When Does 10DLC Still Matter?

If you need to send very high volumes of A2P traffic exclusively to US carriers (e.g., 10+ million messages per month), or require guaranteed delivery via US-originated numbers with full carrier compliance, 10DLC registration may eventually become necessary. Some US carriers also apply stricter filtering to international routes at extreme volumes. SMSRoute can advise on this if your volume grows into that tier — but for the vast majority of use cases, international routing works reliably and at lower cost.
