What is a custom sender ID and why use one?

A custom sender ID is the name or number that appears as the “From” field on a recipient’s phone. Instead of a random long number or a shared pool name, you can use your brand name (e.g., MyStore), a short code, or an alphanumeric string up to 11 characters. This boosts open rates and builds trust, recipients see a recognizable name, not an unknown number.

Common use cases: one-time passwords (OTPs), appointment reminders, delivery notifications, marketing alerts, and two-factor authentication. A branded sender ID makes your message look professional and reduces the chance it gets ignored or marked as spam.

Does every country support custom sender IDs?

Not all countries support custom sender IDs. In markets like the US, Canada, India, Philippines, Australia, and Japan, regulations require sender ID registration or restrict alphanumeric sender IDs entirely. In the US, for example, A2P 10DLC registration is mandatory for application-to-person messaging. India requires DLT registration. Australia and Japan often require pre-approval of sender IDs.

SMSRoute supports custom sender IDs on routes where the local carrier allows them. When you request a custom sender ID, we check the destination country and advise you on feasibility. For countries that require registration, we can guide you through the process or use our smart shared pool as a fallback. We only promise what we can deliver.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use any custom sender ID I want?

You can request any alphanumeric sender ID up to 11 characters. We’ll check if the destination country’s carriers support it. Some countries block certain patterns (e.g., all-numeric IDs that look like phone numbers) or require pre-registration. We’ll advise you on what works and offer alternatives if needed.

Is there a minimum volume to use a custom sender ID?

No minimum volume, no monthly commitment, and no contract. You can send 10 messages or 10 million with the same custom sender ID. Pay only for what you use.

How long does it take to activate a custom sender ID?

Typically within a few hours after your request. If the destination country requires registration (e.g., Australia, Japan), it may take 1–3 business days. We handle the registration process for you.

What happens if a country doesn’t support my custom sender ID?

We fall back to our smart shared pool of sender IDs, which are optimized for deliverability. You can also choose to use a different custom ID that the route supports. You’re notified before any message is sent.

Do you support inbound SMS with custom sender IDs?

Inbound numbers (virtual mobile numbers) are available on request for select countries. These can be used to receive replies to your branded SMS. Contact support for availability and pricing.

Is my custom sender ID shared with other users?

No. Your custom sender ID is exclusive to your account on routes where it’s activated. It is not shared with any other user for the same destination country.
