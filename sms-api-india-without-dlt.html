What Is DLT Registration and Why Does It Matter for India SMS?

DLT (Distributed Ledger Technology) registration is a mandatory compliance requirement by TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India) for any entity sending transactional or promotional SMS using Indian sender IDs (e.g., 6-digit alphanumeric codes). It applies to Indian businesses and local SMS providers. However, international senders—those not registered in India—can still deliver messages to Indian subscribers via routes that do not require DLT, such as international long-distance (ILD) gateways. These routes typically use a shared or generic sender ID (e.g., a random number or short code) and are fully legal for non-Indian entities. Is it legal to send SMS to India without DLT as an international sender?
