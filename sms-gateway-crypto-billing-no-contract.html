SMS Gateway with Crypto Billing & No Monthly Contract – SMSRoute

SMSRoute is the SMS gateway that lets you pay with Bitcoin, Ethereum, USDT, Monero, Litecoin, or Solana — with zero monthly commitments. Fund your wallet, send messages, and get auto-credited for failures. No contracts, no minimums, no identity verification required.

What does “no monthly contract” actually mean for your SMS API?

Most enterprise SMS providers lock you into annual contracts, monthly minimum spends, or volume commitments. If your traffic dips, you still pay. SMSRoute works on a pure pay-as-you-go model: you top up your wallet with crypto (or keep a small balance), send messages, and that’s it. There is no monthly minimum, no contract term, and no penalty for inactivity. Your unused balance is refundable to your originating wallet on request.

How does crypto billing work for SMS?

SMSRoute accepts BTC, ETH, USDT (TRC-20 and ERC-20), XMR, LTC, and SOL. Top-ups are confirmed automatically — no manual approval, no waiting. You do not need a credit card or bank account. Once the transaction is confirmed on-chain, your wallet balance updates instantly. You can then send SMS via REST API or SMPP, and failed or undelivered messages are auto-credited back to your wallet. See the full crypto payment guide.

What are the actual prices?

SMSRoute pricing starts at $0.004 per message for many routes. Premium corridors (e.g., Australia, Japan) range up to $0.035 per message. Coverage spans 149 countries. There are no hidden fees, no monthly platform charges, and no per-number rental costs unless you request dedicated inbound numbers. Check live per-country pricing.

Do I need KYC or business documents?

No. SMSRoute is a no-KYC SMS API. You sign up with just an email address — no passport, no utility bill, no company registration. You can fund your wallet with crypto and start sending messages in minutes. This is especially useful for developers, privacy-conscious projects, and businesses that operate globally without a legal entity in every region. Learn more about no-KYC SMS.

What about reliability and support?

SMSRoute maintains 99.9%+ uptime (trailing twelve months). Delivery uses adaptive multi-route logic with automatic failover. Real-time DLR webhooks and a dashboard let you track every message. Failed messages are auto-credited. Support is available 24/7 via Telegram and email, with typical first response under one hour. You also get free test credits on signup.

Can I use custom sender IDs and handle STOP keywords?

Yes. SMSRoute provides a smart shared sender ID pool by default. Custom alphanumeric sender IDs are available on request where routes support them. Inbound numbers can also be provisioned on request. Built-in STOP-keyword handling and suppression lists are included. Scheduling, bulk sending, and link shortening are all available via the API. Explore the API docs.

Frequently asked questions

Can I pay for SMS with Bitcoin or USDT without a monthly contract?

Yes. SMSRoute accepts BTC, ETH, USDT (TRC-20/ERC-20), XMR, LTC, and SOL. There is no monthly minimum or contract. You fund your wallet, send messages, and auto-credit failures. Unused balance is refundable.

Do I need to register my business or provide ID to use SMSRoute?

No. SMSRoute requires only an email address to sign up. No identity documents, no business verification, no KYC. You can start sending SMS in minutes after funding your wallet with crypto.

What happens if a message fails to deliver?

Failed or undelivered messages are automatically credited back to your SMSRoute wallet. You do not need to file a ticket or dispute. This applies to all routes and all message types.

Is SMSRoute reliable for production use?

Yes. SMSRoute has 99.9%+ uptime over the trailing twelve months, with adaptive multi-route delivery and automatic failover. Real-time DLR webhooks and a dashboard give you full visibility. Support responds typically within an hour.

Can I use a custom sender ID with SMSRoute?

Yes. A smart shared sender ID pool is used by default. Custom alphanumeric sender IDs are available on request where routes support them. Inbound numbers can also be provisioned.

How do I get started with the SMSRoute API?

Sign up at smsroute.cc with your email, fund your wallet with crypto, generate an API key, and send your first message. Code examples for Python, PHP, Go, and Node are on GitHub. Free test credits are provided on signup.
