What Does 'No Verification' Really Mean for an SMS API?

When you search for an SMS API with no verification, most providers mean they still need your phone number, business license, or government ID. SMSRoute is different: no identity documents ever. You sign up with an email address, choose a crypto payment method, and your account is active immediately. There is no KYC step, no business vetting, and no waiting for approval. This is ideal for developers, privacy-conscious users, and businesses that want to send messages without exposing personal or corporate data.

Frequently asked questions

Can I really send SMS without any ID verification?

Yes. SMSRoute requires only an email address to sign up. No identity documents, business license, or phone number verification is ever needed. You fund with crypto and start sending immediately.

What happens if a message fails to deliver?

Failed or undelivered messages are automatically credited back to your SMSRoute balance. You only pay for successfully delivered messages.

Can I get a refund for unused balance?

Yes. Unused balance can be refunded to the originating crypto wallet on request. There are no hidden fees or minimums.

Is SMSRoute suitable for sending OTPs or verification codes?

Absolutely. SMSRoute supports high-delivery routes for OTPs, with real-time DLR webhooks so you can confirm delivery. The no-verification signup means you can integrate OTP sending without exposing your identity.

Do I need a contract or monthly commitment?

No. SMSRoute has no contracts and no monthly minimums. You pay only for what you use, and you can stop anytime.

How does SMSRoute handle privacy compared to Twilio?

SMSRoute does not retain message content after delivery. Since we never collect identity documents, there is no personal data to leak. SMS as a protocol is not end-to-end encrypted, but SMSRoute minimizes data exposure by design.
