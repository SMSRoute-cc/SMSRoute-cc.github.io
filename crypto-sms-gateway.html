What is a crypto SMS gateway?

This space contains a cluster of landing pages that look nearly identical in layout and copy. That is exactly why evaluating on marketing polish alone fails. How does crypto billing on a gateway actually work end-to-end?

Crypto billing works end to end by submitting a deposit request with your chosen coin and amount, then polling the matching payment status endpoint until the network confirms and balance credits.

You call POST /deposits-2 with crypto_currency and amount in the body. The response returns an orderId plus the deposit address and exact sum to send. From there you hit GET /payment/status-2/:orderId on a short interval. Once the chain shows enough confirmations the gateway marks the order paid and adds the funds to your account balance ready for SMS traffic. Accepted coins are BTC, ETH, USDT (TRC-20/ERC-20), XMR, LTC and SOL. Nothing else is required beyond the wallet transaction itself.

On-chain confirmation time is controlled solely by the coin network you picked, not by any SMS provider. A gateway that quotes a fixed minute count for every coin is inventing a number it cannot guarantee. After the balance lands, failed messages are automatically credited back. Unused balance is refundable to the originating wallet on request, so you are never locked into a residual that cannot leave.

The whole loop from funded wallet to first API send sits in minutes once the chain clears. That is the complete billing path: deposit call, status poll, automatic credit, optional refund-to-wallet.

What are the actual sending mechanics once you're funded?

Once funded you POST /sms/send with to, from, message and callback_url, then retrieve status from GET /sms/single/:messageId/status at $0.004 to $0.035 per message across 149 countries.

The body takes the destination number, the sender ID or number you want displayed, the text payload, and an optional callback_url that receives the delivery event as soon as the handset or carrier acknowledges it. The response hands back a messageId immediately. You either wait for the webhook or poll the single-message status endpoint; both return the same real-time DLR states we log in the dashboard. Failed messages credit back automatically so you never pay for undelivered traffic.

Pricing sits at a $0.004 floor on the cheapest routes and climbs through the common $0.009 band up to $0.035 only on expensive destinations such as AU or JP.
