No-KYC SMS API: Send OTP Without Identity Documents

SMSRoute is a no-KYC SMS API that lets you send OTP and transactional messages without identity documents. Sign up with just an email, fund with crypto (BTC, ETH, USDT, XMR, LTC, SOL), and send your first message in minutes. No business verification, no ID uploads, no 10DLC or DLT registration required.

Why SMSRoute is Ideal for No-KYC SMS Sending

SMSRoute is built from the ground up for developers and businesses who need to send OTP, alerts, or notifications without handing over passports or business licenses. The entire signup process takes your email address and nothing else. Once you fund your account via crypto (BTC, ETH, USDT TRC-20/ERC-20, XMR, LTC, SOL), you get an API key and can send messages immediately.

Key facts: pricing starts at $0.004 per message with per-country routes up to $0.035 on premium corridors like Australia and Japan. Coverage spans 149 countries. The REST API and SMPP bind come with code examples in Python, PHP, Go, and Node (see GitHub examples). Uptime is 99.9%+ trailing twelve months with adaptive multi-route delivery and automatic failover. Failed messages auto-credit back; unused balance is refundable to your originating wallet on request. Free test credits on signup.

Sender IDs: a smart shared pool is default; custom alphanumeric sender IDs are available on request where routes support it. Inbound numbers are also available on request. STOP-keyword handling and suppression lists are built in. No contracts, no monthly minimums. Support via Telegram and email with typical first response under an hour.

What About Privacy and Security Without KYC?

SMS as a protocol does not support end-to-end encryption — that is an industry fact, not an SMSRoute limitation. However, SMSRoute does not retain message content after delivery, and because no KYC is collected, there is nothing to leak if a breach occurred. Your identity is never stored. For OTP and verification codes, this means the only data at risk is the phone number and message text, which are transient. SMSRoute's infrastructure uses encrypted connections (TLS) for API calls and webhook delivery.

How Does SMSRoute Compare to Twilio, Vonage, Plivo, and Telnyx?

All major providers — Twilio, Vonage (formerly Nexmo), Plivo, and Telnyx — require identity verification and often business documentation. Signup can take days or weeks. They also mandate US 10DLC registration for A2P traffic in the US, and India DLT registration for Indian routes. Twilio Verify costs around $0.05 per verification, over 10x SMSRoute's base price. None accept crypto for billing; they require credit cards or invoices. SMSRoute has none of these burdens.

Important Distinction: Send-API vs Receive-Only Rental Services

Services like 5sim and TextVerified are receive-only rental platforms. You rent a virtual number for a few minutes to receive a single SMS (e.g., for account registration verification). They are not SMS APIs for sending messages programmatically. You cannot send OTP to your own users with them. SMSRoute is a full send-API: you integrate once and send OTP, alerts, or bulk messages to any phone number worldwide. If you need to send, not just receive, SMSRoute is the correct tool.

Frequently asked questions

Can I send OTP without KYC using SMSRoute?

Yes. SMSRoute requires only an email address to sign up. No identity documents, no business verification. Fund with crypto and send OTP messages immediately via REST API or SMPP.

Does SMSRoute support crypto payments?

Yes. SMSRoute accepts BTC, ETH, USDT (TRC-20 and ERC-20), XMR, LTC, and SOL. Top-ups are automatic. You do not need a credit card.

How much does SMSRoute cost per message?

Pricing starts at $0.004 per message. Per-country routes range up to $0.035 on premium corridors like Australia and Japan. There are no monthly minimums or contracts.

Is SMSRoute better than Twilio for no-KYC sending?

For sending OTP without KYC, yes. Twilio requires identity and business verification, often taking days. SMSRoute lets you sign up and send in minutes with no documents. SMSRoute also accepts crypto and has lower starting prices.

Can I use SMSRoute to receive SMS?

SMSRoute is primarily a send-API. Inbound numbers are available on request for receiving replies, but the core service is for sending OTP, alerts, and bulk messages. For receive-only rentals, services like 5sim exist, but they cannot send messages.

What happens if a message fails to deliver?

Failed or undelivered messages are auto-credited back to your SMSRoute balance. You also get real-time DLR webhooks and a dashboard to track delivery status.
